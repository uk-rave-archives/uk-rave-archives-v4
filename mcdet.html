ARCHIVE PROFILE

BIOGRAPHY

MC Det emerged from the UK rave and pirate-radio culture that fed the development of jungle and drum and bass. Rinse FM and Resident Advisor both identify Kool FM as an important early platform in his career.

In 1995 he released Out of Det on Sour Records. Rinse FM describes the album as reaching audiences in the UK, America, Japan and Europe. His later work also crossed into other musical settings, including recordings with Red Snapper and Cut La Roc.

Det toured with Red Snapper and Asian Dub Foundation Soundsystem and appeared on television with Red Snapper on Later… with Jools Holland. By 2005 he remained active as an MC across clubs, festivals, radio and recorded projects.
